Overview

Fantasy Arena was a new prediction format where users answer multiple questions (like a poll) with money pooled together. Instead of binary yes/no, users predict across 3-4 questions and assign multipliers (3x, 2x) to boost their points on high-confidence picks.

The Format: Answer 3 out of 4 (or 3/3) questions correctly, assign your 3x and 2x multipliers strategically, and compete for the pooled prize.

My Role

  • Designed the multi-question pool mechanics
  • Built the multiplier assignment system (3x, 2x)
  • Created the scoring and leaderboard logic
  • Defined prize distribution rules
  • Iterated based on user feedback and engagement data

How It Worked

  • Multiple Questions: 3-4 prediction questions bundled together around a theme (e.g., a cricket match)
  • Multipliers: Assign 3x to your strongest conviction, 2x to your second ‚Äî amplify your points strategically
  • Pool Prize: All entry fees pooled, distributed based on correct predictions and multiplier usage
  • Engagement: Higher skill ceiling than binary predictions ‚Äî rewarded research and conviction
