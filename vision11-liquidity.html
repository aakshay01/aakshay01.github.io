The Problem

Low liquidity events were hurting new users. In sports categories with thin order books, power users could easily manipulate prices ‚Äî buying up liquidity and moving markets to their advantage. New users entering these markets faced unfair pricing, leading to poor first-trade experiences and high drop-off rates.

This was particularly acute in non-cricket sports like Football, where trading volume was lower but user interest was growing. The result: new users would make their first trade at manipulated prices, lose, and never return.
